About me.

I am a student at MIT (class of 2024) who is pursuing a double major in Mathematics and Computer Science and Engineering (18 and 6-3). I also have a concentration in Music. My general academic interests include many aspects of computational mathematics such as complexity theory, algorithms, machine learning, and quantum computation/information theory.

If you're interested in any of my past or present projects (academic or personal), you can check out my GitHub page. My GitHub is a bit barren, since I don't usually upload projects unless someone wants to check out something.

Even More About Me.

I am a member of the Committe of Math Majors (CoMM). The CoMM is a small, selective group of mathematics undergraduates who dedicate their time to collecting data and creating actionables to improve the math department at MIT. This includes coordinating with faculty members to improve the advising process for both students and faculty advisors. Previously, my focus was on running multiple discussion forums with faculty and students, where we discussed community building and academics within the department. This semester, I worked on maintaining the Mathematics Underground Guide.

I spend most of my free time doing music related stuff. I was a Musical Director for the Asymptones acapella group for a year. I previously held assistant musical director and social chair positions. I also do a lot of arranging for 1-, 4-, and 6-part acapella. I'm working on a solution for sharing my many arrangements online without having to use the MuseScore website.
