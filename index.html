About me.

I am a student at MIT (class of 2024) who is pursuing a double major in Mathematics and Computer Science and Engineering (18 and 6-3). I also have a concentration in Music. My general academic interests include many aspects of computational mathematics such as machine learning, algorithms, complexity theory, and quantum computation/information theory.

If you're interested in any of my past or present projects (academic or personal), you can check out my GitHub page. My GitHub is a bit barren, since I don't usually upload projects unless someone wants to check out something.

Even More About Me.

I am a member of the Committe of Math Majors (CoMM). The CoMM is a small, selective group of mathematics undergraduates who dedicate their time to collecting data and creating actionables to improve the math department at MIT. This includes hosting the Mathematics Underground Guide and coordinating with faculty members to improve the advising process for both students and faculty advisors. Last semester, my focus was on running multiple discussion forums with faculty and students, where we discussed community building and academics within the department.

I spend most of my free time doing music related stuff. I'm a Musical Director for the Asymptones acapella group. I previously held assistant musical director and social chair positions. I also do a lot of arranging for 1-, 4-, and 6-part acapella. I'm working on a solution for sharing my many arrangements online without having to use the MuseScore website.

I also have a good amount of job experience, in case that's what you're here for. Most recently, I've completed an internship at Amazon where I worked on an incredible piece of software used for orchestrating machine learning experimentation workflows. I can't give much details outside of what's on my resume due to a high level of confidentiality. Before that, I did research at NASA JPL. A presentation of my work was given by my mentor at the IGARSS conference in July 2023, but you may need an account to access the paper. You can also read related work where I am listed as a co-author at the Harvard Astrophysics Data System.

I'm also a member of the Musical Theatre Guild (MTG) and participate in productions as much as I can. I was officially involved with Heathers (2023) and Pippin (2022). I've also worked with the Shakespeare Ensemble before as a video editor for their virtual production of Timon of Athens (2021). Most recently I acted in Next Act's production of Remembering Her (2023), an original jukebox musical written by a fellow MIT student.
